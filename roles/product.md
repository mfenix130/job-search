# Role Overlay: Product Manager / Product Leader

Supplemental signals and theater patterns for product management and product leadership roles.
Includes: Product Manager, Senior PM, Staff PM, Group PM, Director of Product, VP Product, CPO.
Load this file alongside the resume-reviewer or resume-tailor skill when the role category is Product.

---

## What Strong PM Resumes Actually Show

- **Outcome ownership:** shipped products with measurable user or business impact — not just features delivered on schedule
- **Discovery depth:** how the problem was identified, validated, and prioritised — not just "gathered requirements"
- **Tradeoff reasoning:** what was cut and why, what was built and why, what the candidate pushed back on
- **Data fluency:** how decisions were made, what metrics were tracked, how success was defined and measured
- **Cross-functional influence:** how engineering, design, legal, sales, and support were aligned — who resisted and how it was resolved
- **Customer proximity:** direct user research, sales calls, support escalations — evidence of first-hand problem understanding
- **Roadmap ownership:** did the candidate build the roadmap or execute someone else's? What changed because of their judgment?
- **Launch ownership:** GTM coordination, rollout strategy, post-launch iteration based on data

## What Weak PM Resumes Do

- List features shipped without showing user or business impact
- Claim discovery and strategy work they facilitated but did not own
- Use "collaborated with" for everything — never showing individual judgment or decisions made
- Describe process improvements ("introduced agile") without showing what changed in outcomes
- State metrics without baselines or context ("increased engagement by 20%")
- Use "drove" and "led" for everything — none of it specific enough to probe
- Claim product vision ownership when they executed a roadmap handed to them

## Theater Patterns to Aggressively Flag

| Phrase | What to probe |
|--------|--------------|
| "Led product strategy for X" | What specifically did you decide? Who disagreed? What did you say no to? |
| "Drove cross-functional alignment" | On what? Who was misaligned and why? What did you give up to get agreement? |
| "Increased engagement by X%" | What was the baseline? What changed? How was engagement defined and measured? |
| "Launched X features" | Which ones actually moved a metric? Which flopped? What did you learn? |
| "Collaborated with engineering and design" | What decision did you make that engineering pushed back on? How did you resolve it? |
| "Defined the product roadmap" | Who approved it? What got cut? What did you fight to keep? |
| "Conducted user research" | How many users? What method? What surprised you? What changed in the product because of it? |
| "Improved NPS / CSAT by X points" | What specifically changed? What was the pre/post measurement methodology? |

## Seniority Calibration

**PM / Associate PM:**
- Owns one feature area or a small product surface
- Executes on a roadmap with guidance from senior PM or Head of Product
- Should show: specific feature ownership, user research involvement, data tracking, cross-functional coordination

**Senior PM:**
- Owns a product area end-to-end — discovery through launch through iteration
- Makes roadmap decisions within their scope; influences adjacent areas
- Should show: outcome ownership, prioritisation decisions, tradeoffs made, cross-functional influence

**Staff PM / Group PM:**
- Owns a product domain spanning multiple teams or surfaces
- Defines the problem space, not just the solution space
- Mentors other PMs, influences PM practice
- Should show: strategic framing, multi-team alignment, PM development, org-level product decisions

**Director of Product / VP Product:**
- Owns the product strategy for a product line or the company
- Operates at business strategy level — influences company direction, go-to-market, pricing
- Manages other PMs; defines how product management is practised at the org
- Should show: company-level product decisions, hiring/developing PMs, revenue or retention impact, executive stakeholder management

## Key Evaluation Areas

**Discovery and Validation:**
How did the candidate know what to build? Interview methodology, data analysis, competitive research, customer proximity. Not just "talked to users" — what did they learn, what changed?

**Prioritisation:**
What framework? What was deprioritised and why? How were engineering effort and user value weighed? What happened when stakeholders disagreed?

**Metrics and Success Definition:**
What was the north star? What leading indicators? What happened post-launch vs. the hypothesis? Did the PM track it themselves or rely on analysts?

**Technical Fluency:**
Not coding — but can the candidate have a real conversation with engineers about tradeoffs, feasibility, and technical debt? Evidence: technical decisions influenced, architecture discussions participated in.
