# Role Overlay: Risk & Compliance / GRC

Supplemental signals and theater patterns for risk, compliance, and governance roles.
Includes: Risk Manager, Compliance Officer, GRC Analyst, Regulatory Affairs, Internal Audit, CISO-adjacent.
Load this file alongside the resume-reviewer or resume-tailor skill when the role category is Risk & Compliance.

---

## What Strong Risk & Compliance Resumes Actually Show

- **Regulatory specificity:** which frameworks (SOC2, PCI-DSS, HIPAA, GDPR, DORA, FFIEC, NIST, ISO 27001), what the candidate's ownership of them looked like, not just that they "managed compliance"
- **Audit evidence quality:** who was the auditor, what was the scope, what did the candidate produce vs. coordinate
- **Risk quantification:** risk appetite definition, loss exposure estimates, likelihood/impact methodology — not just "identified risks"
- **Remediation ownership:** did the candidate track findings to closure or hand them off? What was the SLA?
- **Cross-functional influence:** how was risk and compliance communicated to business leaders, engineers, legal? What changed because of it?
- **Control design:** did the candidate design controls or implement existing frameworks? There's a big difference.
- **Regulatory relationship management:** exam preparation, regulator interaction, MRA/MRIA handling
- **Written artifacts:** policies, standards, procedures, risk assessments — evidence of authorship quality

## What Weak Risk & Compliance Resumes Do

- List frameworks as skills without showing depth of application
- Claim ownership of compliance programs they supported or contributed to
- Describe annual audit preparation without showing what they actually produced
- Use "improved risk posture" with no measurement or evidence of change
- Conflate awareness training with actual risk reduction
- Claim "board-level reporting" when they prepared slides someone else presented
- List every acronym (COSO, COBIT, NIST, ISO) without showing command of any of them

## Theater Patterns to Aggressively Flag

| Phrase | What to probe |
|--------|--------------|
| "Led SOC2 / ISO 27001 compliance program" | Type I or Type II? What was your scope vs. external auditor's scope? What evidence did you produce? |
| "Managed enterprise risk framework" | What methodology (COSO, ISO 31000)? How was risk scored? Who approved the risk appetite? |
| "Improved compliance posture" | Measured how? Before/after on what metric? Open findings count? Audit rating? |
| "Partnered with regulators" | Which regulator? What was the nature of the interaction? MRAs? Exams? Corrective action? |
| "Delivered board-level risk reporting" | Did you present, or prepare? What decisions did the board make based on it? |
| "Implemented controls for [regulation]" | Which control domains? Who tested them? What was the evidence artifact? |
| "Managed vendor risk program" | How many vendors? What was the tiering criteria? What happened when a high-risk vendor failed? |
| "Drove policy and procedure development" | Who approved them? How were they operationalized? What changed in employee behavior? |

## Seniority Calibration

**Compliance Analyst / Risk Analyst:**
- Executes control testing, evidence collection, policy maintenance within a defined program
- Should show: specific frameworks, evidence quality, tracking discipline, control testing methodology

**Senior Compliance / Risk Manager:**
- Owns a compliance domain or risk category end-to-end
- Designs the assessment process, not just runs it
- Manages relationships with business lines and external auditors
- Should show: program design, examiner/auditor relationships, findings-to-closure ownership, cross-functional influence

**Director / Head of Compliance / Chief Risk Officer:**
- Sets risk appetite and compliance strategy for the organization
- Reports to board/audit committee, manages regulatory relationships
- Owns the overall compliance posture, not a domain within it
- Should show: strategic decisions, regulatory exam outcomes, org-level policy authority, business trade-off navigation

## Domain-Specific Signals

**Financial Services:**
BSA/AML, OFAC, CCAR, DFAST, FFIEC, OCC/Fed/FDIC exam experience, MRA/MRIA handling, model risk management

**Healthcare:**
HIPAA, HITRUST, state-specific requirements, breach notification procedures, BAA management, audit trail integrity

**Technology / SaaS:**
SOC2 Type II, ISO 27001, GDPR/CCPA data subject rights, vendor due diligence, bug bounty policy, third-party risk

**Government / Defense:**
FedRAMP, FISMA, NIST 800-53, clearance relevance, ATO process, CMMC

## Key Differentiator

The highest-value compliance and risk professionals reduce actual risk, not just audit findings. 
Probe whether the candidate's work changed how the business operated, or just what the paperwork said.
